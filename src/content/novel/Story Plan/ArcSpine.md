\#\#\# Arc / Spine (Beat Sheet)

\#\#\#\# Five Overarching Arcs

\*\*Arc 1: The Fugitive\*\*

\- \*\*Central Question:\*\* Can you take one irreversible step, even if you don't feel ready?

\- Rain seemingly running away from his problems that have been thrust upon him, but really he’s avoiding the stuff in himself, the introspection. He is forced to be portrayed as a fugitive as a result of a singular small commitment to being uncomfortable (making a decision to change the status quo of his life; aka break the cycle of his commute for a moment to explore the ghost).

\- This mirrors the act of going your own path in the sense that the people that are with you often don’t see the true potential or really the necessary action of letting go of the life they perceive is life. People will be concerned for your safety and will keep questioning your decisions until you “succeed” and prove them wrong. While that is true, by the end of the story, it’s simply to prove to yourself that you must live within that discomfort.

\*\*Arc 2: The Mirror (Asylum)\*\*

\- \*\*Central Question:\*\* Can you trust someone who seems to be everything you wish you were?

\- An abandoned asylum that’s been repurposed as a refuge for the broken. Establishes Vesper’s kindness and showcases how effective his methods (not fully revealed that he is manipulating/removing memories from people). But, it’s actually very dystopian and by the end of the arc, he betrays the group. Likely here he will cause Roxana to be effectively paralyzed (her body loses the memory of being able to stand or have tenacity) and will cause Claire to follow him due to her goals/philosophy (the surefire high probability of solving her existential threat, perhaps a terminal illness or something else, not sure). Both Rain and Roxana are captured.

\- The patients/refugees we see in the asylum are people that are happy and seem to feel free. They don’t have any random or defining irrational quirks making them a bit more forgettable. They do stuff to make the asylum more of a sanctuary, but only through tasks and never making things on their own accord. They seem content and are stuck in the bubble that is the asylum.

\- A key thing to note is how tragic the actual details of each of the patients we see in the asylum are, but they never seem to be really burdened by it anymore. It should show just how technically correct their removal of the pains/struggles are; that they are seemingly high functioning and do plenty of good now. But what’s lost is subtle, the human core is gone and in a way, it’s like they are NPCs designed to make the asylum look good rather than being actual people building their own stories.

\- This puts into question whether letting people get these struggles removed is truly a good thing. It is a good thing in terms of a utilitarian view or strictly “moral” good idea, but it is a subtle disgrace against the beauty of humanity. Yet, how could I convince people who have all these traumas and such to see they have the potential to act in spite these things? I don’t think there’s any possible answer that I could impart; that it is only through lived experience that you can prove to yourself it’s possible. Even though it is never guaranteed and could lead to death/ruin anyways.

\*\*Arc 3: The Prisoner\*\*

\- \*\*Central Question:\*\* Can you let go of every other self you could have been and choose to be singular?

\- An intrusive and claustrophobic cell with deprivation pods designed to pacify the deviants/troublemakers in society. Rain faces all of his internal struggles and truly sees them visually within his mind. He must kill the self that likes infinite potential, so he kills every single possibility of his selves until only he himself remains. Once he triggers his own awakening, I think his passive ability ghost will play a role in activating Roxana’s ability remotely to escape the deprivation tanks. It acts on his own will subconsciously and reaches for the nearest person to connect with. By it appearing to Roxana, she is able to... \*(spark flame without artifact)\*.

\*\*Arc 4: The Witness (Circuit Line)\*\*

\- \*\*Central Question:\*\* Can you keep choosing a decision that's already been made, when your own habits pull you backward and no one around you is walking at your pace?

\- Looping transit to give an opportunity for the party to figure out a plan or what to do. They have arguments on if they should pursue Vesper as on paper he is doing stuff that is truly saving society. Opportunity to reveal parts of each character like what Roxana faced in the deprivation chamber and Claire’s secret (illness?).

\- The Devil's Bargain is offered: a shortcut to stabilize the water without struggle. Rain finds his voice and refuses. The Perfect Clone may manifest here or in Arc 5.

\- The group decides to face Vesper together — a community forged in the long middle.

\*\*Arc 5: The Proof (Caldera)\*\*

\- \*\*Central Question:\*\* Can you act with your real self, even when the old self is still screaming, and keep choosing it every day?

\- The group fully commits to chasing down Vesper and stopping him from enacting forced correction onto the populace.

\- Final confrontation: Vesper offers to remove the Split. Rain refuses. He sets aside the Clone, writes the first line. The Split whispers. He writes the second line. Triggers a controlled eruption; the water loop breaks; the rain falls clean.

\- Roxana fills the lighter with her own flame. Claire makes the final call without the Click. Shiori stays, remembering without notes.

\- Vesper is left with the prism, his philosophy cracked.

\- Final image: Rain's notebook open, two lines written, the rest blank. Or Rain's hand pointing forward, palm up, thumb open. \*In spite of it all, I choose to be Rain.\*

\#\#\#\# Key Plot Beats

\- Rain sees an afterimage of his clone seemingly leading him towards a different path on his commute. This decision alone leads him to the inciting events of the story.

\- Vesper seemingly acts differently during certain parts of the story. In terms of foreshadowing: he is forgetful of recent events/things after a recent memory extraction and will casually react to things based on memories he has already extracted from people. This will be based on the memory glass shards he’s collected up during the events of the story.

\- Vesper is revealed to have the ability to extract memories and has done this for his own goals. He betrays the group; extracting Roxana’s important decisive memory of her past to cause her to physically be paralyzed. Claire is horrified of her mistake of trusting Vesper? Claire may have been assisting Vesper’s plans unknowingly due to her want to extract a memory that has plagued her mind throughout her life.

\- Both Rain and Roxana are trapped within the sensory deprivation prison that’s designed to pacify inmates. Rain goes through a trial of facing his base core self.

\- A scene that shows Rain on top of a mountain of bodies, bodies of himself, with him bloodied. Implies he had to kill all of his selves (potential selves) to become himself.

\- Vesper is practically unbeatable by Rain not only due to having so much accumulated memories inside him, but the fact that Roxana’s core memory was extracted, which allows Vesper to fully predict Rain’s combat maneuvers as he was primarily trained by Roxana.

\- Rain screws up and gives Vesper an opportunity to extract a memory of his. This causes an internal trial to occur where Rain faces his past. He is at a decision point of whether to accept the...

\- Upon finding your true self, you are truly living in the present. The climax has Rain's true self giving the final hint without words. He becomes quiet and listens to his soul.
