Theme: A self is built through irreversible commitment. Avoiding commitment — staying in the realm of infinite potential — is a slow self-erasure.

Antithesis: Relief is real. Removing pain can feel like freedom. Sometimes it might even be better — for a moment, for some people, in the short term. But it hollows the self over time.

Central Motif: 

Memory - A record of the self and is a proof of commitment. Removing it removes your identity. 

Shared Sub Motifs: 

Rain / Water - Commitment as something you let fall on you rather than to avoid. Accumulation. Flow vs Stagnation.

Falling - An irreversible act that can happen unexpectedly or through acceptance. Sometimes, you need to let yourself fall and take a risk.

Character Specific Motifs

Glass - Vesper’s view on memory. A fragile piece of a person that can be extracted to reform/shape them into a “better” person.

Noise / Silence - Claire’s overthinking intrusive mind vs the peace of certainty. Commitment quiets the noise temporarily.
