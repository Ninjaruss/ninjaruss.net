\#\#\# Rain Azure?

\#\#\#\# Identity & Role

\- Abstract Role: Heart

\- Tag: The Paralyzed Dreamer

\- Main Trait: Procrastinator

\- Passive Trait: Dreamer

\- Age: 23

\- Height: 5'7"

\#\#\#\# Abilities

\- \*\*Passive: Ghost\*\*

\- Afterimage visible only to Rain.

\- Appears at thresholds between commitment and retreat.

\- Repeats a single gesture: step forward, open hand.

\- Shows authentic desire, not safe path.

\- Rain has spent years misreading it.

\- Does not require artifact.

\- Does not react to the Clone.

\- The Ghost is a faded, translucent gold — the color of a promise not yet claimed.

\- \*\*Active: Clone\*\* (awakens during inciting incident)

\- Intangible, distinguishable copy.

\- Performs single action based on intent.

\- Requires goggles (artifact) to activate.

\- Cost: Sensory deprivation scaling with duration. Blurred vision → ringing ears → blindness/deafness → collapse. Short-term memory loss in extreme use. Recovery: 10–30 seconds after dismissal.

\- The Clone can create an illusionary copy of a thrown object as part of its single action. The object appears to fly and strike but causes no physical effect. Intangible, silent, vanishing on contact or shortly after.

\- The Clone flickers with unstable gold light — action borrowed, not owned.

\- \*\*Advanced: Perfect Clone\*\* (awakens near climax)

\- Indistinguishable from Rain. Still intangible.

\- No immediate sensory cost.

\- Deferred cost: Each use deepens the distinctiveness of his internal procrastinator voice; it grows more autonomous and persuasive, becoming the Split. His old self, refusing to die quietly.

\- Only Rain’s base Clone transforms; he cannot go back.

\- On its first appearance, the Perfect Clone pulses a single, solid gold — beautiful, but dead. The false shine of perfected stasis.

\- Consequences only hinted at; to be explored in a future arc/sequel.

\*His active ability involves sending a proxy of himself to do an action he’d like to do, but is intangible and cannot actually do anything, which relies on him to do the actions himself. This is an expression of the procrastinator trait.\*

\*While his passive ability is the ghost that guides him towards what he truly wants to do in spite of any risks or consequences. This is an expression of the dreamer trait.\*

\#\#\#\# The Split (Internal Voice of Avoidance)

\- The Split is Rain’s internal voice of doubt and avoidance, grown distinct through his reliance on the Clone and his history of deferring commitment.

\- It evolves across the story: from quiet thought to persistent habit to a voice with personality to an internal presence he argues with, culminating in the Perfect Clone — a physicalized version of himself that offers to take over entirely.

\- The Split is not malevolent. It genuinely believes it is protecting Rain from the pain of failure and commitment. It is the part of him that perfected avoidance as a survival strategy.

\- Rain’s victory is not destroying the Split. It is recognizing it as part of himself, absorbing its fears, and acting with his own hand regardless. He writes the first line. The Split whispers. He writes the second.

\#\#\#\# Character Chain

\- History: Gifted praise, discouraged dreams, waiting for permission.

\- Interpretation (Lie): \*\*My worth comes from my infinite potential, not from anything I actually do. The moment I commit to one path, I become finite – and finite means I might fail.\*\*

\- Motivation (Fear & Fix):

\- Fear: That choosing one finite path will prove he’s not special and will kill all his other potential selves.

\- Fix: Avoid commitment by staying in preparation mode, seeking perfect certainty, and using proxies (the clone) to act at a distance.

\- Behavioral Pattern: Analysis, waiting, using clones as proxy, retreating into comfort. Dislikes ordinary tasks and defers them until they need to be addressed.

\- Boundary: “I will not let anyone else define my story or erase my memories.”

\- Collision: Follows the ghost without permission, becomes a fugitive from him taking action, forced to act without certainty.

\- Parents: My dad makes his own assumptions and doesn't actually listen. My mom is protective, worried about security. Rain's parents are not villains. They are hardworking, fearful people who equate security with love. His father makes assumptions and doesn't listen. His mother worries constantly and offers support that feels generic—"you can do it" without understanding what "it" is. They want him to be safe. He wants to be seen. These are not the same thing. Their voices echo in his need for authenticity: the part of him that needs external permission before acting. He learned to deflect because deflecting was safer than explaining himself to people who couldn't hear him. As a result, the story puts them more of a distant role that still has an affect over Rain primarily through phone calls and texts from them.

\#\#\#\# Layers

\- Layer 1 (Immediate): “I’m fine.” Quiet, polite, deflective. Slightly dark, transparent.

\- Layer 2 (Situational): Requires authenticity - cannot act until the choice feels truly his. Avoids commitment, uses the Clone as proxy. Mid-bright, obscure.

\- Layer 3 (Foundational): Believes his potential is his truest self. The untested dream is perfect; committing might prove he’s ordinary. Carries a silent notebook. Very bright, very obscure.

\#\#\#\# Artifact: Goggles

\- Found near the location of the inciting incident.

\- Wearing them sharpens the appearance of the Ghost.

\- Does not inherently improve his vision; lenses replaced during the story to fit his prescription (near sighted).

\- His artifact. Replaces his ordinary glasses after the inciting incident. Sharpens the Ghost's signal and, once fitted with prescription lenses, corrects his ordinary vision. The old glasses are left behind—a small, irreversible act. The goggles become part of his silhouette, a permanent marker of the self who chose to follow.

\- Possible memory: Goggles worn by a worker who refused to continue working in spite of his whole life built by his work? Not sure; needs further exploration.

\#\#\#\# Signature Gesture

\- \*\*Index finger pointing forward. Middle, ring, and pinky fingers closed. Thumb open. Palm facing upward.\*\*

\- Initially used to summon the Clone as a command — a standard, functional point.

\- After Arc 3, the gesture shifts unconsciously: the thumb opens, the palm rotates upward. It becomes an invitation, not an order.

\- By Arc 5, he uses the gesture without summoning the Clone — simply pointing at what he has chosen.

\- Full-body stance: feet planted, shoulders square, gaze toward the target, other arm relaxed at his side. The whole body agrees with the hand.

\#\#\#\# Physical Tells

\- Benign hand tremor: A faint, persistent shake. Worsens at thresholds — when a decision looms, when the Ghost appears, when someone asks him what he actually wants. The body refusing to commit.

\- Withdrawn hands: Right hand holds his left forearm behind his back. His habitual resting posture; the grip is light, comfortable. The dominant hand — the one that could write, throw, or reach — is literally holding him back from action. Whereas the ghost is often seen to reach out with its palm upward, like it’s inviting others (or himself) to reach out and grab it.

\- Nervous laughter + follow-up: A short, breathy laugh at the wrong moment, immediately followed by a self-deprecating excuse or admission. "Heh. Sorry, I'm just—I'm overthinking it." The laugh is the reflex. The follow-up is the recovery — acknowledging the awkwardness before anyone else can.

\- Processing gaze: When genuinely engaged, his eyes drift to the middle distance — not avoidance, but visualization. He's turning words into images, thinking. When he's done, his eyes return, and he speaks. The people close to him learn to wait through the drift.

\- Deflective lock-in: Direct eye contact, polite half-smile, and a partial truth: "I'm fine." "Just tired." The words are honest enough to pass, but the full truth is withheld. A deliberate mask. The steady gaze sells the concealment.

\#\#\#\# Signature Objects

\- Spinning a pen: Carries a pen for utility, but rarely writes with it. Spins it through his fingers, tosses it in the air, catches it. A fidget that keeps his hands busy without producing anything. The tool of creation, always in motion, never used for its purpose.

\- Notebook of half finished ideas: A collection of started-and-abandoned projects — self-analysis, doodles, game ideas. He's creative and curious, but never finishes anything.

\#\#\#\# Appearance

\- Mole on the left hand: A small dark mole on the top of his left hand, sitting between the middle and index fingers. A quiet, permanent landmark on the hand that trembles, the hand the world sees while the dominant hand hides.

\- Wavy, unkempt hair: Naturally wavy, dark, slightly too long. Rarely styled. He runs his hand through it when anxious, making it messier. Often tucked under his cadet cap. Over the course of the story, it grows longer—at first from neglect, then from intention. By the end, the length is a choice. The same hair that was once a sign of avoidance becomes a quiet act of self-definition.

\- Goggles: His artifact. Replaces his ordinary glasses after the inciting incident. Sharpen the Ghost's signal and, once fitted with prescription lenses, correct his ordinary vision. The old glasses are left behind—a small, irreversible act. The goggles become part of his silhouette, a permanent marker of the self who chose to follow.
