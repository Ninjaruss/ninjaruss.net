Main Trait: Reformer

Passive Trait: Martyr

“The Reforming Martyr”


| Layer | Expression | Bright/Dark | Transparent/Obscure |
| :----- | :----- | :----- | :----- |
| Layer 1 (Immediate) | Calm, reasonable, helpful. "I can help you become who you're meant to be." | Bright | Transparent |
| Layer 2 (Situational) | Extracts memories to correct flaws. Reshapes identity by removing what he judges to be broken.  When someone resists his perspective, he reframes their resistance as confusion. "You're not ready yet."  | Dark | Semi-Transparent |
| Layer 3 (Foundational) | Mother was someone who never committed. Her formlessness caused irreversible harm. Vesper fears he is the same. His reform of others is proof to himself that he is different — he acts, he changes things, he is not her.  But the one memory he won't melt is hers. It's the evidence. Without it, he might forget who he's trying not to become  | Very dark | Very obscure |


Vesper’s combat style is that of a composite fighter. He is able to switch between styles based on the memories of those he’s extracted. Occasionally, echoes of the person’s memory appears briefly for the receiving end, almost as if they see an entirely different person in Vesper for a split second.
