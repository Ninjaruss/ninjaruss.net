Passive: Ghost

\- Afterimage visible only to Rain.

\- Appears at thresholds between commitment and retreat.

\- Repeats a single gesture: step forward, open hand.

\- Shows authentic desire, not safe path.

\- Rain has spent years misreading it.

\- Does not require artifact.

\- Does not react to the Clone.

Active: Clone (awakens during inciting incident)

\- Intangible, distinguishable copy.

\- Performs single action based on intent.

\- Requires goggles (artifact) to activate.

\- Cost: Sensory deprivation scaling with duration.

Blurred vision → ringing ears → blindness/deafness → collapse.

Short-term memory loss in extreme use?

Recovery: 10–30 seconds after dismissal.

\*The Clone can create an illusionary copy of a thrown object

as part of its single action. The object appears to fly and

strike but causes no physical effect. Intangible, silent,

vanishing on contact or shortly after.

Advanced: Perfect Clone (awakens near climax)

\- Indistinguishable from Rain. Still intangible.

\- No immediate sensory cost.

\- Deferred cost: Each use deepens the distinctiveness of his internal voice (procrastinator self-talk); turning into an almost separate character that grows more distinct, persuasive, and autonomous. His old self, refusing to die quietly.

\- Consequences only hinted at; to be explored in a future arc/sequel

\*Only Rain’s base Clone transforms into Perfect Clone; he cannot go back?

His active ability involves sending a proxy of himself to do an action he’d like to do, but is intangible and cannot actually do anything, which relies on him to do the actions himself. This is an expression of the procrastinator trait.

While his passive ability is the ghost that guides him towards what he truly wants to do in spite of any risks or consequences. This is an expression of the dreamer trait.
