\#\# Physical Tells

Benign hand tremor

A faint, persistent shake. Worsens at thresholds — when a decision looms, when the Ghost appears, when someone asks him what he actually wants. The body refusing to commit.

Withdrawn hands

Right hand holds his left forearm behind his back. His habitual resting posture; the grip is light, comfortable. The dominant hand — the one that could write, throw, or reach — is literally holding him back from action.

Whereas the ghost is often seen to reach out with its palm upward, like it’s inviting others (or himself) to reach out and grab it.

Nervous laughter + follow-up

A short, breathy laugh at the wrong moment, immediately followed by a self-deprecating excuse or admission.

"Heh. Sorry, I'm just—I'm overthinking it."

The laugh is the reflex. The follow-up is the recovery — acknowledging the awkwardness before anyone else can.

Processing gaze

When genuinely engaged, his eyes drift to the middle distance — not avoidance, but visualization. He's turning words into images, thinking. When he's done, his eyes return, and he speaks. The people close to him learn to wait through the drift.

 	

\#\# Behavioral Patterns

Deflective lock-in

Direct eye contact, polite half-smile, and a partial truth: "I'm fine." "Just tired." The words are honest enough to pass, but the full truth is withheld. A deliberate mask. The steady gaze sells the concealment.

 	

\#\# Signature Objects

Spinning a pen

Carries a pen for utility, but rarely writes with it. Spins it through his fingers, tosses it in the air, catches it. A fidget that keeps his hands busy without producing anything. The tool of creation, always in motion, never used for its purpose.

Notebook of half finished ideas

A collection of started-and-abandoned projects — self-analysis, doodles, game ideas. He's creative and curious, but never finishes anything.

 	

\#\# Appearance Details

Mole on the left hand

A small dark mole on the top of his left hand, sitting between the middle and index fingers. A quiet, permanent landmark on the hand that trembles, the hand the world sees while the dominant hand hides.

Wavy, unkempt hair

Naturally wavy, dark, slightly too long. Rarely styled. He runs his hand through it when anxious, making it messier. Often tucked under his cadet cap. Over the course of the story, it grows longer—at first from neglect, then from intention. By the end, the length is a choice. The same hair that was once a sign of avoidance becomes a quiet act of self-definition.

Goggles

His artifact. Replaces his ordinary glasses after the inciting incident. Sharpen the Ghost's signal and, once fitted with prescription lenses, correct his ordinary vision. The old glasses are left behind—a small, irreversible act. The goggles become part of his silhouette, a permanent marker of the self who chose to follow.
